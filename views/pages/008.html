I know I have decided to venture into the lands of Linux out of my own volition (partially out of spite for Windows, actually...) and will most likely (leaving that 0.01% for doubt here!) never turn back, but the lingering feeling remains... Most of us Linux citizens had been Windows users in the past, raised on Microsoft's products like the Office suite and DOS. We have fled with discontent, but home sickness is sure to strike us when times are dire. Some of us hate Microsoft with passion, though there are things we honestly miss about Windoze. Just to name a few:

  • Simple, yet colorful themes with the ability to change the colors of almost anything.
  • Gaming classics like Monkey Island, Arcanum, The Settlers or Age of Empires
  • Clear and intuitive GUIs
  • The blue screen of death (BSOD) - kidding, of course!

Some time ago a small team of Russian programmers decided to start a project named ReactOS, whose goal is to create a free operating system fully compatible with the older NT 5 architecture (Windows 2000/XP). So far the project is clearly in its alpha stage (version 0.3.17 was recently mentioned on DistroWatch), but the developers claim it is already quite functional. I myself am very much looking forward to its progress and I sincerely hope it succeeds. It would be nice to see a combination of the best of Windows and Linux worlds coming together...
